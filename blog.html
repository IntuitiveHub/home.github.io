My programming journey (2017-present)


I was captivated by the creating and creation of applications when I was 10-11 years old, when kids my age were engrossed in playing computer games, and I always wondered how these things worked, i.e. how these types of games are made.

To discover more about behind-the-scenes of games, I searched on my computer for how to construct your own game and found a website called Sploder, back when Adobe Flash was still in use. As a result, I began creating my own platformer games using the website's built-in themes.

Scratch was introduced to me in fifth grade in 2018. It was a programming language taught at our school, and I wanted to know more about it than what was in our textbook (because our book just gave us basic knowledge about it rather than going into details). I created a Scratch account and began building and publishing projects.

I used to spend hours coding, experimenting, and learning from YouTube videos (especially Learn Learn Scratch Tutorials and Griffpatch). I made several platformer games, animations, and so on. I am unfortunately left behind with no evidences anymore as I deleted my previous accounts :P

Due to COVID, I had a lot of free time after online classes so I used to code in Scratch. My father decided to book online coaching for me to learn more about app and web development in 2020, after learning about my growing interest in computer science and aiming to persue Software Engineering. So he scheduled a WhiteHatJr demo session but to, be honest, the mentor didn't teach me anything new. So he enrolled me in a Wizklub batch, which was far superior to WhiteHatJr; I learned Blockly , how to code games, how to construct web-applications, how to code an Alexa skill, and so on.
